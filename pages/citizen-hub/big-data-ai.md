# Big data and artificial intelligence

## On this page
- [Introduction](#introduction)
- [AI in healthcare](#ai-in-healthcare)
- [Bias, fairness and accountability](#bias-fairness-and-accountability)

Artificial intelligence (AI) and big data have the power to revolutionise health and medicine. They can support more accurate diagnoses, help clinicians make decisions, and improve the overall quality of care. At the same time, it is essential to ensure that these technologies promote fair and patient-centred care, and do not reinforce — and ideally help to reduce — existing biases and inequalities in genomics and healthcare.

Their benefits can be significant, but so can their risks, many of which are difficult to measure or predict. Because these technologies will inevitably shape our future, their ethical and societal implications need to be discussed openly with the public.

## AI in healthcare

In medicine, AI systems can, for example, detect patterns in medical images (such as tumours) or generate summaries of conversations between doctors and patients. Such tools may enable faster, more reliable diagnoses.

However, a major challenge is the “black box” nature of many AI systems: it is often unclear how they arrive at their conclusions. This raises important questions about accuracy and accountability. If an AI-supported decision leads to an error, who is responsible — the developer, the clinician, or someone else?

To maintain trust in AI-supported healthcare, human oversight will remain essential.

## Bias, fairness and accountability

Ideally, big data and AI could make healthcare more efficient, cost-effective, and accessible to underserved populations. Yet AI tools can also be biased because they are trained on datasets that may be incomplete or unrepresentative — for example, by lacking data from certain groups.

As a result, their output may be less accurate or less useful for these populations. There are ways to reduce such biases and to use AI to create a fairer healthcare system, but doing so requires careful ethical and societal reflection.

These conversations are crucial to ensure that big data and AI genuinely contribute to better health for everyone.
